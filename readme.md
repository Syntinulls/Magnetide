# Magnetide — Master Pitch

## One-liner
A 2D side-scroller salvage roguelite where you run a hovering platform with a giant magnet, haul up valuable junk from a trash planet, and defend your rig from trash-beasts, other scavengers, and various environmental hazards—then return to orbit to cash in and upgrade for the next run.

## High concept
You’re on a salvage team stationed above a trash planet. During a run, you operate a hovering salvage platform equipped with a massive magnet that drags floating debris past/under your platform. You decide what to keep, what to bin, and what to discard—balancing value, risk, and limited storage.

Pulling up debris can disturb “trash beasts” that attack your magnet and cargo. You must defend the platform by shooting off attackers and keeping the magnet intact.

When the run ends (end condition TBD: magnet destroyed vs time limit), you return to an orbiting space station to process salvage and upgrade your kit—magnet strength/durability, platform defenses, personal magnet (used to grab items off the main magnet and move them to storage), and storage capacity. Then you drop back in for another run.

## Player fantasy
- Be the scrappy space salvager who turns garbage into profit.
- Feel the satisfying “fishing” pull of the magnet and the quick triage decisions.
- Survive escalating swarms while protecting a fragile, valuable machine.

## Core gameplay loop
1. **Salvage run:** pull up trash as it floats by your platform.
2. **Triage:** retrieve items and choose to **store**, **bin**, or **discard**.
3. **Threat response:** disturbing trash beasts triggers attacks—**shoot/defend** to protect the magnet/cargo.
4. **Run end:** magnet destroyed or time limit (WIP).
5. **Return to station:** process items → earn money.
6. **Upgrade:** magnet/defenses/personal magnet/storage.
7. Repeat with higher stakes and better gear.

## Key systems (initial)
- Giant magnet “catch” surface that accumulates debris
- Item triage: keep/bin/discard
- Storage constraints and value vs risk decisions
- Enemy swarms (“trash beasts”) that target the magnet
- Player combat/shooting to defend the rig
- Station hub: processing + upgrades

## Open questions / WIP
- Run end condition: time limit vs magnet destruction vs both
- Scoring: profit only vs profit + efficiency + risk bonuses
- Progression: roguelite meta upgrades vs run-to-run loadouts
- How items vary: common junk vs rare tech vs cursed/hostile items

## Premise (source)
You are part of a salvage team. Your job is scouring over a trash planet on a hovering platform equipped with a giant magnet. It is a 2D side-scroller where you pull up trash that floats along by your platform. You can retrieve this trash and choose to bin or discard it. However, pulling up trash can disturb certain trash beasts that will attack your magnet, which you must defend. You can shoot to fight off these monsters and protect your cargo. Once a run ends (either your magnet is destroyed or the time limit runs out, this is still WIP), you return back to the space station orbiting the planet to process your salvaged items, where you can upgrade your equipment (i.e. the magnet, your defenses, or your personal magnet which is used to pickup the items off the larger magnet and move them to your storage, or the storage itself). This process of doing salvage runs, fighting off swarms of monsters, and returning with your precious cargo is the heart of the game, where you then turn it in for a pretty penny, to upgrade your setup and go in again.
